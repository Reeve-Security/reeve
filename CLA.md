# Reeve Contributor License Agreement (CLA)

Thank you for contributing to Reeve. To keep the project legally clean for
everyone who relies on it, all contributors must agree to this Contributor
License Agreement before their contribution can be merged.

This is a lightweight, industry-standard agreement (modeled on the Apache
Software Foundation Individual/Corporate CLA). It does **not** transfer
ownership of your work. You keep the copyright to what you write. It only
grants the project the rights it needs to use and redistribute your
contribution under the project's [Apache-2.0 license](LICENSE).

The Owner of the project for the purposes of this agreement is **Reese Skye LLC**
("the Owner"). (Reeve is a product of Reese Skye LLC.)

## How signing works

Signing is enforced automatically: when you open a pull request, a CLA bot
(EasyCLA / CLA Assistant) checks whether you have signed and, if not, posts a
link to sign. Your PR cannot merge until the check passes. You sign once; it
applies to all future contributions.

## 1. Definitions

- **"You"** means the individual or legal entity making a Contribution.
- **"Contribution"** means any original work of authorship (code, docs,
  configuration, tests) that You intentionally submit to the project (e.g. via
  a pull request, patch, or issue), excluding anything you clearly mark "Not a
  Contribution."

## 4. Your representations

You represent that:

1. You are legally entitled to grant the above licenses.
2. Each Contribution is **Your original creation** (or You have rights to submit
   it and have noted any third-party licenses or restrictions).
3. If You contribute on behalf of an employer, You have permission to do so, or
   Your employer has signed a Corporate CLA covering Your Contributions.

## 5. No warranty / no obligation

You are not expected to provide support for Your Contributions, and unless
required by law, You provide Your Contributions "AS IS" without warranties of
any kind. The Owner is under no obligation to accept or merge any Contribution.

## 6. Corporate contributions

If You are contributing as part of an organization, an authorized signer for
that organization should sign the Corporate CLA (handled by the same CLA bot),
which lists the employees authorized to contribute on the organization's behalf.
